Tennis

I picked up tennis in sixth grade as the first sport I ever tried. Instantly, I fell in love with the rhythm of the game, the sound of the ball meeting the strings, the satisfying screech of sneakers against the court, and the quiet tension that filled the air during each rally. Over time, tennis became more than just a sport to me as it became a lesson in patience and resilience. The court taught me that success isn’t about perfection but the willingness to keep playing the next point and to keep showing up even when your serves keep missing or your opponent seems unbeatable. I learned to think strategically, to read my opponent’s movements, and to keep calm under pressure. Even now, I carry those lessons off the court. Whether I’m facing academic deadlines or personal challenges, I remind myself of the same mindset I had before every match: focus, adapt, and keep moving forward. Tennis didn’t just build my athletic ability but also shaped the way I look at life. I will always hold the lessons that I had gained from my lessons learning tennis and use them to shape my development as I go into future endeavors.
